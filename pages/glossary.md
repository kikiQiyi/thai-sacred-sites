## A

### Amulet
A small religious object believed to offer protection, blessing, luck, or spiritual power. In Thailand, Buddhist amulets are often collected, worn, exchanged, and sold in specialized markets.

Examples: Phrachan Market; Pantip Amulet Market

## B

### Buddha Image
A visual representation of the Buddha used in worship, meditation, temple decoration, and devotional practice. Buddha images may appear as large temple statues or smaller objects sold in markets.

Examples: Wat Rong Suea Ten; Mae Sai Border Market

## C

### Chinese-Thai Shrine
A shrine connected to Chinese religious traditions as practiced in Thailand. These shrines often include incense, candles, deity statues, offerings, and ancestor or deity veneration.

Example: Chao Pho Yi Ko Hong Shrine

## D

### Donation Box
A container placed in temples or shrines for visitors to give money. Donations may support temple maintenance, monks, rituals, or merit-making practices.

Examples: Wat Rong Suea Ten

## I

### Incense
A ritual object burned during prayer or offering. Incense smoke is commonly associated with devotion, communication with deities or ancestors, and sacred atmosphere.

Example: Chao Pho Yi Ko Hong Shrine

## M

### Merit-Making
A Buddhist practice of generating merit through good actions, donations, offerings, temple visits, or support for monks and religious institutions.

Examples: Donation boxes; offering sets

## O

### Offering
An object given in a religious setting, such as flowers, candles, incense, food, drinks, cloth, or prepared offering baskets. Offerings express devotion, gratitude, prayer, or merit-making.

Examples: Wat Rong Suea Ten; Chao Pho Yi Ko Hong Shrine

## S

### Shrine
A sacred place dedicated to a deity, spirit, ancestor, or religious figure. Shrines may be independent spaces or located within larger urban, temple, or commercial environments.

Example: Chao Pho Yi Ko Hong Shrine

## T

### Temple
A religious site used for worship, ritual, teaching, community gathering, and pilgrimage. In this collection, most temples are Buddhist sites in northern Thailand.

Examples: Wat Rong Suea Ten; Wat Saeng Kaew Phothiyan; Wat Phra That Doi Wao

## W

### Wat
The Thai word for a Buddhist temple or monastery. A wat may include shrines, Buddha images, monks’ residences, donation spaces, ritual areas, and architectural decorations.

Examples: Wat Rong Suea Ten; Wat Phra That Doi Wao
